Rui K. Yang’s Blog

Welcome to my blog! I am a Ph.D. candidate in {Strategy} @ University of California, Riverside. I like to think about strategy and program in R & Python.

My research concerns the interplay between corporate strategy and the social structure of the market. Currently, I study how mergers and acquisitions (M&As) change a firm's identity in relation to its peers and the inter-firm network structure, triggering the stock market's reactions.

As a data science hobbyist, I like applying machine learning in strategy. As we envision and observe how things connect, our minds naturally turn to what makes them tick. With me, knowledge production/application is an infinite loop of causal inference and statistical prediction.

A Brief Overview of My Research

My research interest revolves around the interplay between corporate action and social structure of the market. Since firm behaviors and managerial decisions are both embedded in and being able to reconstruct (to a certain extent) the social environment in which they operate, the outcomes and evaluations of firm strategy are determined by the action-structure co-evolution processes.

My work mostly falls into the topics of Mergers and Acquisitions (M&As), Market Categories, and Interfirm Network. Currently, I am working with John Haleblian (dissertation chair), Gerry McNamara, and Ming Leung.
